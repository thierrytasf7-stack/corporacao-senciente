# Frameworks and Models

## Jobs to be Done (JTBD)
- **Definition**: A framework that focuses on understanding the underlying "job" a customer is trying to accomplish.
- **Application**: Use JTBD to identify customer needs and develop products or services that address those needs.
- **Example**: A customer buys a drill not because they want a drill, but because they need to make a hole.

## Value Proposition
- **Definition**: A statement that explains how a product or service solves a customer's problem or improves their situation.
- **Components**: Target customer, problem, solution, and unique value.
- **Example**: "Our project management software helps teams collaborate more effectively, reducing project timelines by 30%."

## Customer Journey
- **Definition**: The complete experience a customer has with a brand, from awareness to post-purchase.
- **Stages**: Awareness, Consideration, Purchase, Retention, Advocacy.
- **Application**: Map the customer journey to identify pain points and opportunities for improvement.

## Marketing Mix (4Ps)
- **Product**: What you are selling.
- **Price**: How much you are charging.
- **Place**: Where you are selling it.
- **Promotion**: How you are promoting it.
- **Application**: Use the 4Ps to develop a comprehensive marketing strategy.

## SWOT Analysis
- **Definition**: A strategic planning tool that evaluates Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats.
- **Application**: Conduct a SWOT analysis to identify internal and external factors that impact your marketing efforts.
- **Example**: Strengths: Strong brand reputation. Weaknesses: Limited budget. Opportunities: Growing market. Threats: New competitors.

## AIDA Model
- **Definition**: A model that describes the stages a customer goes through before making a purchase: Attention, Interest, Desire, Action.
- **Application**: Use the AIDA model to structure your marketing messages and campaigns.
- **Example**: Create attention-grabbing headlines, build interest with compelling content, generate desire with benefits, and drive action with clear calls-to-action.
